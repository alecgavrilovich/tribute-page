Milutin Milanković

1879 - 1958

Milutin Milanković (Милутин Миланковић, 28 May 1879 – 12 December 1958) was a Serbian mathematician, astronomer, climatologist, geophysicist, civil engineer, doctor of technology, university professor and popularizer of science. Milanković gave two fundamental contributions to global science. The first contribution is the Canon of the Earth’s Insolation, which characterizes the climates of all the planets of the Solar system. The second contribution is the explanation of Earth's long-term climate changes caused by changes in the position of the Earth in comparison to the Sun, now known as Milankovitch cycles (11 Ice Ages). This explained the ice ages occurring in the geological past of the Earth, as well as the climate changes on the Earth which can be expected in the future.

Important years

  • 1879 born in Dalj, Austria-Hungury(modern Crotia).
  • 1896 finished secondary school in Osijek.
  • 1902 graduated from Vienna University of Technology with best marks.
  • 1904 at age of 25 he gained Ph.D., his thesis was entitled Contribution to the Theory of Pressure Curves (Beitrag zur Theorie der Druck-kurven)
  • 1909 became a teacher at University of Belgrade.
  • 1912 published "Contribution to the mathematical theory of climate"
  • 1913 published "Distribution of the sun radiation on the earth's surface".
  • 1914 published "About the issue of the astronomical theory of ice ages".
  • 1916 published "Investigation of the climate of the planet Mars".
  • 1919 return to Belgrade.
  • 1920 publiched a book "Mathematical Theory of Heat Phenomena Produced by Solar Radiation".
  • 1924 became a full member of Serbian Academy of Sciences and Arts.
  • 1934 published a book "Celestial Mechanics".
  • 1939 began work on "Canon of Insolation of the Earth and Its Application to the Problem of the Ice Ages", it was publish on Serbian and German during the war.
  • 1954 received the Golden Doctor's diploma from the Technical University of Vienna.
  • 1958 suffered a stroke and died in Belgrade, He is buried in his family cemetery in Dalj.
